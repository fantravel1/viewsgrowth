Traffic Sources

Definition

Traffic Sources is an Analytics report showing how viewers discover your videos. Understanding traffic sources helps optimize content strategy for each discovery pathway.

Main Traffic Sources

Browse Features

Views from homepage, subscription feed, and other browsing surfaces. Indicates algorithm promotion and subscriber engagement.

Suggested Videos

Views from suggestions shown alongside other videos. Usually the largest source for established channels.

YouTube Search

Views from search results. High-intent traffic indicating good SEO performance.

External

Views from external websites, social media, and embeds. Indicates off-platform promotion success.

Channel Pages

Views from your channel page. Often existing subscribers exploring your content.

Playlists

Views from playlists (yours and others). Can significantly boost watch time.

Analyzing Traffic Sources

  • High Browse: Algorithm favors your content
  • High Search: Strong SEO, evergreen potential
  • High Suggested: Content chains well with others
  • High External: Good promotion strategy

Traffic Source Goals

A healthy mix varies by channel type, but generally:

  • News/trending: High browse and external
  • Tutorial/how-to: High search
  • Entertainment: High suggested and browse
