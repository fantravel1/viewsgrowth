Long-Form Content

Definition

Long-form content refers to YouTube videos typically over 10 minutes, allowing for mid-roll ads, deeper topic exploration, and higher watch time per video. Contrasts with Shorts (under 60s).

Long-Form Advantages

  • More ads: Videos 8+ min can have mid-roll ads (previously 10 min)
  • Higher RPM: More ad placements = more revenue per view
  • Watch time: More minutes per video helps algorithm ranking
  • Topic depth: Thoroughly cover complex subjects
  • Authority: Establishes expertise in your niche

When to Use Long-Form

  • Educational tutorials and how-tos
  • Documentary-style content
  • Reviews and comparisons
  • Storytelling and vlogs
  • Podcasts and interviews

Long-Form Best Practices

  • Earn every minute—no padding
  • Use chapters for navigation
  • Pattern interrupts maintain engagement
  • Tease value throughout to keep viewers
  • Place mid-roll ads at natural breaks

Long-Form vs Shorts Strategy

Many successful creators use both: Shorts for discovery and reach, long-form for monetization and depth. Shorts can funnel viewers to long-form content.
