Watch Page

Definition

The watch page is the YouTube page where videos play. Includes the video player, title, description, comments, and suggested videos. Where most viewer engagement happens.

Watch Page Elements

Video Player

The main player showing your video. Includes controls for playback, volume, quality, captions, and fullscreen. End screens appear in last 20 seconds.

Title & Metadata

Video title, view count, upload date, and hashtags. First thing viewers see below player.

Engagement Buttons

Like/dislike, share, save, and more options. Engagement signals affect algorithm recommendations.

Channel Info

Channel name, avatar, subscriber count, and subscribe button. Key area for converting viewers to subscribers.

Description

Expandable section with video description, links, and chapters. "Show more" reveals full content.

Comments

Viewer discussion area. Engagement here signals active content. Pinned comments are powerful for CTAs.

Suggested Videos (Sidebar/Below)

Related videos recommended by the algorithm. Major traffic source for other creators.

Desktop vs Mobile

  • Desktop: Sidebar suggestions, larger player, more visible description
  • Mobile: Suggestions below, smaller text, description hidden by default

Optimization Tips

  • Front-load important info in description (before "Show more")
  • Pin important comment for visibility
  • Use cards to keep viewers on your content
  • Optimize for both desktop and mobile viewing
