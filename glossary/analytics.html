Data & Insights

Analytics

Definition

YouTube Analytics is the data dashboard within YouTube Studio that provides detailed insights into your channel and video performance, audience behavior, traffic sources, and revenue data.

Analytics Tab Overview

Overview Tab

High-level snapshot of channel performance:

  • Views, watch time, and subscribers
  • Top videos by views
  • Real-time views (last 48 hours)
  • Latest video performance comparison

Content Tab

Individual video and content type analysis:

  • Performance by video, Shorts, live, posts
  • Impressions and CTR per video
  • Average view duration comparisons
  • How viewers find your content

Audience Tab

Who's watching your content:

  • Unique viewers and returning viewers
  • When your viewers are on YouTube
  • Demographics (age, gender, geography)
  • Other channels your audience watches
  • Subscriber vs non-subscriber breakdown

Revenue Tab

For monetized channels only:

  • Estimated revenue
  • RPM (Revenue per mille)
  • CPM (Playback-based CPM)
  • Transaction revenue (Super Chat, memberships)
  • Top earning videos

Key Metrics to Monitor

Click-Through Rate (CTR)

Percentage of impressions that become views. Indicates thumbnail/title effectiveness. Benchmark: 4-10% is good.

Average View Duration (AVD)

Mean time viewers spend watching. Critical for algorithm. Compare to video length for percentage retention.

Audience Retention Graph

Visual representation of when viewers leave. Identify drop-off points to improve future content structure.

Traffic Sources

Where views originate: Search, Suggested, Browse, External. Reveals what's working.

Impressions

How often your thumbnails are shown. High impressions with low CTR = thumbnail needs work.

Using Analytics for Growth

Weekly Analytics Review

  1. Compare CTR of recent videos—replicate high performers
  2. Check audience retention—note where viewers drop off
  3. Review traffic sources—double down on what's working
  4. Study "When your viewers are on YouTube"—schedule accordingly
  5. Analyze top-performing videos—create similar content

Analytics Tips

  • Wait 48-72 hours: Data stabilizes after initial algorithm testing
  • Compare to your averages: Not industry benchmarks
  • Use date ranges: Last 28 days for trends, 90 days for patterns
  • Export data: Download for deeper analysis in spreadsheets
  • Check Advanced Mode: More detailed breakdowns available
