Cards (Info Cards)

Definition

YouTube Cards are interactive elements added to videos that appear as small rectangles viewers can click. They promote other videos, playlists, channels, polls, or external links (for eligible creators).

Types of Cards

Video Card

Links to another video. Use to promote related content and increase session time.

Playlist Card

Links to a playlist. Great for driving binge-watching sessions.

Channel Card

Promotes another channel. Used for collaborations and shoutouts.

Poll Card

Interactive poll for viewers to vote. Boosts engagement and provides audience insights.

Link Card

External website link. Only available to YPP members. Used for merchandise, Patreon, etc.

How to Add Cards

  1. Open YouTube Studio
  2. Select your video → Editor
  3. Click "Cards" in the timeline
  4. Choose card type and content
  5. Set timing (when card appears)
  6. Save changes

Card Best Practices

  • Timing: Place when you mention related content
  • Verbal CTA: Tell viewers to click ("Check out the card above")
  • Relevance: Only link truly related content
  • Max 5 cards: Per video, don't overwhelm viewers
  • Avoid early cards: First 20% of video has highest drop-off

Cards vs End Screens

  • Cards: Appear during video, any timestamp
  • End Screens: Only in final 5-20 seconds

Use both strategically—cards for mid-video promotion, end screens for post-video action.
