Monetization

AdSense

Definition

Google AdSense is the advertising platform that enables YouTube Partner Program members to earn revenue from ads displayed on their videos. It handles ad serving, reporting, and payments for YouTube creators.

How YouTube AdSense Works

  1. You join the YouTube Partner Program
  2. Link an AdSense account to your channel
  3. Enable monetization on eligible videos
  4. YouTube serves ads on your content
  5. You earn revenue based on CPM and RPM
  6. AdSense pays you monthly (if threshold met)

Payment Details

  • Threshold: $100 minimum to receive payment
  • Payment Date: Between 21st-26th of each month
  • Methods: Direct deposit, wire transfer, check
  • Revenue Split: YouTube keeps 45%, you get 55%

AdSense vs. YouTube Studio Revenue

Where to Check Earnings

While AdSense handles payments, YouTube Studio provides more detailed revenue analytics. Use Studio for:

  • Daily estimated revenue
  • Per-video earnings breakdown
  • RPM and CPM metrics
  • Revenue by traffic source

Maximizing AdSense Revenue

  • Increase watch time: More ad opportunities
  • Videos 8+ minutes: Enable mid-roll ads
  • High CPM niches: Finance, business, technology pay more
  • Audience geography: US/UK/CA viewers have higher CPMs
  • Avoid demonetization: Follow advertiser-friendly guidelines
