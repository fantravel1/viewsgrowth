Velocity

Definition

Velocity refers to how quickly a video accumulates views after publishing. High velocity signals to the algorithm that content is resonating, triggering broader promotion.

Why Velocity Matters

  • Algorithm signal: Fast views = interesting content
  • Trending potential: High velocity can lead to Trending placement
  • Snowball effect: Early momentum builds more momentum
  • First 48 hours: Critical window for velocity

Factors Affecting Velocity

  • Subscriber base: More subscribers = larger initial audience
  • Notification bells: Immediate alerts drive early views
  • Upload timing: When your audience is online
  • CTR: Compelling thumbnail/title drives clicks
  • Promotion: External traffic, social shares

Velocity vs Total Views

  • High velocity, moderate total: Trending/timely content
  • Low velocity, high total: Evergreen content building over time
  • High both: Viral success

Boosting Initial Velocity

  • Announce uploads on Community Tab
  • Share immediately on social media
  • Use Premieres to concentrate viewing
  • Email list notifications
  • Cross-promote from other videos
  • Publish when audience is most active

Velocity Expectations

What's "good" velocity varies dramatically by channel size and niche. Compare to YOUR previous videos, not other channels. A 10% increase in first-hour views compared to your average is meaningful progress.
