External Traffic

Definition

External traffic refers to views originating from outside YouTube, including social media platforms, websites, direct links, and embedded videos. Valuable for initial video momentum and audience diversification.

External Traffic Sources

  • Social Media: Twitter/X, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Reddit
  • Websites: Blog embeds, news articles, forums
  • Direct: Shared links via email, messaging apps
  • Search Engines: Google (non-YouTube), Bing

Why External Traffic Matters

  • Provides initial velocity for new uploads
  • Diversifies traffic beyond algorithm dependency
  • Brings new audiences who may subscribe
  • Strong external signals can trigger algorithmic promotion

Maximizing External Traffic

  • Share new videos across all social platforms
  • Build email list for direct notification
  • Embed videos in relevant blog posts
  • Share in niche communities (Reddit, Discord, forums)
  • Create platform-native content that links to full videos
