Browse Features

Definition

Browse Features is a traffic source category in YouTube Analytics that includes views from the homepage, subscription feed, and other browsing surfaces where videos are recommended to viewers.

What's Included in Browse Features

Homepage

Videos recommended on YouTube's main page based on viewer history, preferences, and trending content. The largest potential audience.

Subscription Feed

Where subscribers see your new uploads. Not all subscribers see every video—engagement matters.

Trending

Curated list of popular rising videos. Requires significant velocity to appear.

Watch Later / History

Saved videos and previously watched content accessed through browse.

Optimizing for Browse Features

  • Strong thumbnails: Must stand out among many options
  • High CTR: Algorithm tests videos on small audiences first
  • Good retention: Videos that keep viewers watching get promoted
  • Consistent posting: Trains algorithm and subscribers
  • Engagement signals: Likes, comments, shares matter

Browse vs. Search vs. Suggested

  • Browse: Passive discovery, viewers browsing
  • Search: Active intent, viewers looking for specific content
  • Suggested: Recommended alongside currently watching videos

Mature channels often get 40-60% of traffic from Browse Features.
