Frequently Asked Questions

What is the ideal YouTube title length?

The ideal YouTube title length is 40-60 characters. YouTube truncates titles after about 70 characters on desktop and 50 characters on mobile. Keeping titles between 40-60 characters ensures your full title is visible while providing enough room to include keywords and hooks.

What makes a good YouTube video title?

A good YouTube title includes: a number or statistic, power words that create emotion (ultimate, secret, proven), the main keyword near the beginning, and creates curiosity without being clickbait. Questions and how-to formats also perform well. The best titles promise a clear benefit to the viewer.

Should I use numbers in my YouTube title?

Yes, using numbers in YouTube titles increases click-through rates by 15-25%. Odd numbers like 7, 11, or 13 perform slightly better than even numbers. Specific numbers (e.g., "17 tips" vs "many tips") add credibility and set clear expectations for viewers.

Should I put keywords at the beginning of my title?

Yes, placing your main keyword in the first 30 characters improves both SEO and visibility. YouTube's algorithm pays special attention to the beginning of titles, and viewers scanning search results will see your keyword even if the title is truncated.
